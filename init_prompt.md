You are an expert full-stack developer and UI/UX designer. Build a beautiful, responsive, single-page web application for a 31-day devotional tracker called "Faith That Shows Up." The app needs to be highly polished, feature a clean modern design, utilize a tabbed navigation structure, and be ready for deployment to Vercel. 

### Core App Requirements & Technical Stack
1. Framework: Next.js (App Router), React, Tailwind CSS, Lucide React icons, and Shadcn UI (or similar clean component primitives).
2. Local Storage Sync: Because this will be used by a couple ("User 1" and "User 2") over 31 days, create a local state persistence layer (using localStorage) so progress is saved in the browser as they go. 
3. User Profiles: At the top or side of the app, allow toggleable or dual check-off states so both partners can check off each day independently (e.g., "His Progress" and "Her Progress", or two distinct checkbox indicators per day). 
4. Tabbed Architecture: Split the dashboard layout into clear tabs:
   - "Overview": Showing the core F-A-I-T-H strategy and general progress bars for both users.
   - "Weeks 1–5": Five distinct tabs (or sub-tabs) displaying the respective days, scriptures, reflection questions, and checkboxes.
   - "Journal": A dedicated section where they can view the weekly journal prompts and type/save notes locally.

### UI/UX Styling Directions
- Theme: Calm, spiritual, modern aesthetic. Use soft, warm earthy green accents (matching #708A50-style tones) and deep teal tones (matching #2E8B83-style tones) contrasted against clean slate or off-white backgrounds.
- Elements: Use clean rounded cards, smooth transitions when tabs change, responsive layouts for mobile viewing, and satisfying micro-animations when a item is checked off.

#### WEEK 1: UNDERSTANDING FAITH
Theme: A devotional guide for understanding what faith is and placing your trust in God's character.
- Day 1: Hebrews 11:1-6 | Reflection: How does this definition of faith challenge your current understanding of "seeing is believing"?
- Day 2: Romans 10:9-17 | Reflection: What practical steps can you take to surround yourself with more of God's Word this week?
- Day 3: Proverbs 3:5-6 | Reflection: Is there an area in your life right now where you are relying on your own understanding instead of God's?
- Day 4: Ephesians 2:8-9 | Reflection: How does knowing that faith is a free gift—not something you must earn—change your relationship with God?
- Day 5: Mark 11:22-24 | Reflection: When you pray, do you honestly expect God to answer, or are you just going through the motions?
- Day 6: 2 Corinthians 5:7 | Reflection: What would your day look like today if you truly made choices based on faith rather than what you see around you?
- Day 7: Psalm 9:9-10 | Reflection: Think back to a time God proved Himself trustworthy to you. How can that memory strengthen your current faith?
- Week 1 Journal Prompts:
  1. Write an honest evaluation of the current state of your faith. Where do you find it easiest to trust God, and where is it hardest?
  2. Write a prayer asking God to build your foundation on His unchanging character rather than your changing circumstances.
